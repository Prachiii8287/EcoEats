Feeding Futures, Fighting Wastes

EcoEats addresses the urgent issue of food waste in India, where 68.7 million tonnes of food is wasted annually, equating to about 50 kg per person. This waste represents a significant economic loss, amounting to nearly 89,000 crore/year, or almost 1% of the GDP. Additionally, 50% of municipal solid waste in India is organic, indicating a dire need for effective waste management solutions. EcoEats offers a comprehensive approach by facilitating surplus food redistribution and promoting organic waste composting. Through these initiatives, EcoEats aims to combat food waste, enhance food security, and promote sustainability in India.

Surplus Food Donation

In EcoEats, surplus food donation is streamlined, connecting providers directly with underserved communities. Through our platform, surplus food finds its way to those in need, facilitated by transparent transactions and efficient distribution, revolutionizing food donation.

Organic Waste Reduction

EcoEats incentivizes organic food waste reduction through token rewards. Users earn tokens for composting, redeemable for incentives, ensuring transparency and accountability with blockchain technology.
